User research

Interviews, focus groups, user observations, ethnography, user group forums - face to face meetings are essential for understanding the humans targeted as users of a product

Focus group meeting at Altibox where rapidly changing TV habits were the topics of conversation

The value of meeting the people we design for cannot be underestimated. The temptation to assume things about our users is no doubt strong. Even the most experienced designers sometimes fail miserably when making assumptions.
User research can take many forms, depending on the product we are creating. Face to face conversations tend to be the most effective ways of connecting. Often I have invited target users to group meetings, which also seems to engage people in fruitful conversations.
